Turkish Fir

Tall timber, shelter or specimen tree. Great Christmas tree, needles sticking upwards revealing the silvery undersides of scented needles. It performs best in full sun and does not like heavy soil conditions. Evergreen. Hardy.


Growth Rate: Slow
Height after 5 years: 2 m
Height when mature: 15 m

Autumn Blaze (Maple)

Syn. Acer 'Jeffers Red'. A medium-sized tree with a dense crown, ideal as a specimen tree or for amenity planting such as avenues etc. The fresh green leaves change to vibrant shades of red, orange and scarlet over autumn. Wind-tolerant. Makes a stunning shelter belt. Deciduous. Hardy.

Growth Rate: Medium
Height after 5 years: 4 m
Height when mature: 10 m



Acacia Pravissma (Ovens Wattle)

Unusual triangular leaves, profuse golden-yellow flowers in September on a small, spreading tree or large shrub. Fast-growing shelter on dry sites. Tolerates coastal sites. Evergreen. Good frost tolerance.


Growth Rate: Rapid
Height after 5 years: 4 m
Height when mature: 4 m

Abies Vejari (Silver Fir)

An evergreen fir with an upright habit. These dramatic conical shaped conifers make a great statement in the landscape, long-lived and withstanding the elements. Very useful timber tree. Noted as one of the fastest-growing firs in New Zealand. Cold hardy.

Growth Rate: Medium
Height after 5 years: 3 m
Height when mature: 25 m



ABIES NORDMANNIANA SSP. EQUI-TROJANI (Trojan Fir)

A tall pyramidal tree, endangered in the wild. Forms an elegant conical spire of tiered limbs sweeping downwards, with deep glossy green foliage banded white underneath. The most admired of all firs. With age, the tree has smooth grey checkered bark. Female cones are a rich purple brown and 13cm long at maturity. Evergreen. Hardy

Growth Rate:Slow, Medium
Height after 5 years:3 m
Height when mature:25 m
